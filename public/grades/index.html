Our Grades

Learn about the different grades offered at Trostsville Primary School, from Grade R to Grade 7, and what each grade entails.

Our Grades

At Trostsville Primary School, we offer a comprehensive educational journey from Grade R to Grade 7. Each grade is designed to provide age-appropriate learning experiences that foster academic, social, and emotional development.

Overview of Our Grade Structure

Grade R

Age: 5-6 years

Children explore learning through themed units that integrate all learning areas in an engaging, hands-on environment.

Our Grade R program provides a play-based introduction to formal schooling, focusing on foundational skills development. Through structured play, children develop early literacy, numeracy, and social-emotional skills that prepare them for Grade 1.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Phonological awareness and letter recognition
  • Number concepts and counting
  • Fine and gross motor skill development
  • Social skills and classroom routines
  • Creative expression through art, music, and movement

Grade 1

Age: 6-7 years

The reading journey begins in earnest with systematic phonics instruction and guided reading groups.

Grade 1 marks the transition to formal learning with a strong emphasis on literacy and numeracy foundations. Students begin reading, writing, and basic mathematical operations while continuing to develop through play-based activities.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Reading and writing development
  • Addition and subtraction fundamentals
  • Life skills and environmental awareness
  • Independent work habits
  • Continued development of fine motor skills

Grade 2

Age: 7-8 years

Students begin writing their own stories and solving more complex mathematical problems.

In Grade 2, students build on their foundational skills with more complex reading, writing, and mathematical concepts. There is an increased focus on comprehension, creative writing, and problem-solving.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Reading fluency and comprehension
  • Expanded vocabulary and sentence structure
  • Mathematical operations and problem-solving
  • Introduction to formal assessments
  • Personal and social responsibility

Grade 3

Age: 8-9 years

The culmination of Foundation Phase includes standardized assessments to ensure readiness for Grade 4.

Grade 3 completes the Foundation Phase, consolidating literacy and numeracy skills in preparation for the Intermediate Phase. Students develop greater independence and critical thinking skills.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Reading comprehension and analysis
  • Extended writing in different genres
  • Multiplication, division, and fractions
  • Basic research skills
  • Collaborative project work

Grade 4

Age: 9-10 years

Students begin using textbooks and specialized materials for different subjects.

Grade 4 marks the beginning of the Intermediate Phase, with a transition to subject-specific learning. Students encounter more complex content across subjects and develop stronger analytical skills.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Introduction to subject specialists
  • More complex text analysis
  • Advanced mathematical concepts
  • Natural sciences and social studies
  • Time management and organizational skills

Grade 5

Age: 10-11 years

Project-based learning becomes more prominent, encouraging deeper exploration of topics.

In Grade 5, the curriculum expands with more detailed content in each subject area. Students develop stronger research skills and begin more substantive project work.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Literature study and response
  • Fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Scientific investigation methods
  • Historical inquiry and geographical concepts
  • Technology integration across subjects

Grade 6

Age: 11-12 years

Interdisciplinary projects connect learning across different subject areas.

Grade 6 continues building on subject knowledge with increased academic rigor. Students develop stronger critical thinking skills and begin to make connections between different subject areas.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Advanced reading and writing across genres
  • Algebraic thinking and geometry
  • Systems of the human body and earth sciences
  • Ancient civilizations and world geography
  • Digital literacy and online research

Grade 7

Age: 12-13 years

Grade 7 culminates with a capstone project that demonstrates mastery of skills across subject areas.

As the final year of primary school, Grade 7 prepares students for the transition to high school. The curriculum focuses on consolidating knowledge and developing the skills needed for future academic success.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Critical analysis of texts and media
  • Pre-algebra and advanced geometry
  • Laboratory experiments and scientific method
  • Independent research projects
  • Leadership and responsibility

Academic Approach

Our curriculum follows the national guidelines while incorporating innovative teaching methods that cater to different learning styles. We emphasize:

  • Strong foundation in literacy and numeracy
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving skills
  • Creativity and self-expression
  • Physical development and healthy living
  • Social skills and emotional intelligence

Assessment and Progression

We believe in continuous assessment rather than relying solely on examinations. Throughout the school year, learners are assessed through:

  • Class participation
  • Projects and assignments
  • Tests and quizzes
  • Practical demonstrations
  • Standardized assessments

Parents receive regular feedback about their child’s progress through termly report cards and parent-teacher meetings.

Special Programs

In addition to our core curriculum, we offer various programs to enrich the learning experience:

  • Remedial Support: Additional assistance for learners needing extra help
  • Gifted Program: Enhanced learning opportunities for high-performing students
  • Cultural Activities: Music, art, and drama integrated into our curriculum
  • Sports Development: Various sporting disciplines offered throughout the grades

School Day Structure

Our school day is structured to balance academic learning with physical activity and creative expression:

  • Morning Assembly: 7:30 AM - 7:45 AM
  • First Session: 7:45 AM - 10:00 AM
  • Break: 10:00 AM - 10:30 AM
  • Second Session: 10:30 AM - 12:30 PM
  • Lunch: 12:30 PM - 1:15 PM
  • Third Session: 1:15 PM - 2:30 PM
  • After-School Activities: 2:45 PM - 4:00 PM (varies by day)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average class size?

We maintain a maximum of 30 learners per class to ensure quality attention to each child.

How are learners placed in classes?

Placement is primarily age-based, but we also consider social dynamics and learning needs.

Are there subject specialists for higher grades?

Yes, from Grade 4 onwards, learners have subject specialists for different learning areas.

What language of instruction is used?

English is our primary language of instruction, with Afrikaans taught as a second language.

How do you handle the transition between grades?

We have a comprehensive transition program that prepares learners for the increased responsibility and academic demands of each new grade.
